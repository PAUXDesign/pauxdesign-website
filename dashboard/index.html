How I think

Most designers open Figma and start with the interface. I start with the users and the organisation: who makes the decisions, where the friction is, what the constraints are, and what will actually get built.

I focus on AI-driven B2B SaaS, enterprise tools and mobile products — the kind of complex systems where good UX has real impact and bad UX has real consequences.

My background in graphic design and people operations shapes how I work: visual craft, systems thinking, and a deep understanding of how organisations actually run.

How I work · methodology

From research to shipped product.

A look at how I approach a project end-to-end. The same 4 phases I bring to every team — adapted to your sprint cadence, design system and stack.

1 · DiscoverPhase 1
Kick-off & alignment

Understand product goals, users, technical constraints and team rituals.

OUTPUTbrief
Heuristic audit

Review product surface, key flows and any existing design system.

OUTPUTfindings
Stakeholder map

Who decides, who builds, who's blocked. Where the friction lives.

OUTPUTFigJam board
2 · DefinePhase 2
User research

Generative interviews, surveys or analytics review — synthesised into themes.

OUTPUTinsights
IA & user flows

The bones. Where every screen lives, what it does, how users move.

OUTPUTsitemap · flows
Hypotheses & metrics

What we're testing, what success looks like, how we'll know.

OUTPUTspec
3 · DesignPhase 3
Wireframes

Low-fi to validate logic and IA before any pixels are pushed.

OUTPUTFigma
Hi-fi UI

Production-ready screens, tokens, components, states and motion.

OUTPUTdesign system
Usability testing

Maze or moderated tests. Iterate based on what users actually do.

OUTPUTtest report
4 · ShipPhase 4
Dev handoff

Specs, motion, edge cases. Pair with engineers throughout build.

OUTPUThandoff
QA & polish

Review staging until it matches Figma. No drift, no compromises on detail.

OUTPUTQA log
Post-launch review

Look at the metrics. What worked, what to iterate, what ships next.

OUTPUTretrospective
How I collaborate
  • Embed with the team — async-first, sync when it matters
  • Pair closely with PMs, engineers and stakeholders
  • Communicate trade-offs early and clearly
  • Document decisions in Figma, Notion or wherever the team lives
  • Bring tools (Figma · Maze · Cursor · Claude) but adapt to yours
What I bring to a team
  • End-to-end ownership — research through dev handoff
  • Systems thinking from a graphic-design + people-ops background
  • Comfort with regulated, complex domains (compliance, HR, legal)
  • AI/LLM UX patterns — making model behaviour visible and trustworthy
  • HTML/CSS literacy — I can read what engineers ship and align to it
